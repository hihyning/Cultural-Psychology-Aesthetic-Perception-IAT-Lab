About This Project

This interactive web experiment was developed as part of my personal and academic research informed by LPSY 3026 at The New School. It explores how individuals categorise visual and written information in real time and your inputs will contribute to my ongoing research into how culture and cognition shape our instinctive responses to design.

Practice Round 1: Categorise Images

In this practice round, you will be shown a series of visual design images, one at a time.

Your task is to categorise each image as either representing an Eastern or Western design style based on your best judgment.

Please respond as quickly and accurately as possible.

Use the following keys:

Press E for EAST
Press I for WEST

The next image will appear automatically after each response.

This round is for practice only.
Your data will not be recorded at this stage.

EAST WEST

Practice Round 2: Categorise Words

In this next practice round, you will be shown a series of words, one at a time.

Your task is to categorise each word as either GOOD or BAD based off their connotations as quickly and accurately as possible.

Use the following keys:

Press E for GOOD
Press I for BAD

The next word will appear automatically after each response.

This round is for practice only.
Your data will not be recorded at this stage.

GOOD BAD

Trial 1

In this trial, you will categorise a mix of images and words.

Use the following keys:

Press E for EAST or GOOD
Press I for WEST or BAD

The next item will appear automatically after each response.

Please respond as quickly and accurately as possible.
Your data will be recorded from this point onward.
